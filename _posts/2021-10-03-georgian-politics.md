https://www.reuters.com/world/europe/georgia-ruling-party-takes-lead-local-voting-amid-political-crisis-2021-10-03/ alright pretty simple really -- georgia is now a complete parliamentary democracy, similar to the UK (they used to have a largely ceremonial president who was directly elected, but recently, they changed the constitution to make the president completely ceremonial and chosen by parliament)

historically, they used to be one of the soviet socialist republicans, after being annexed in 1922, nothing too horrific in the soviet union stands out, barring the usual corruption, probably partially because stalin was ethnically georgian. georgia's economy did pretty well under the soviet union. they seceded in 1991 (as it was collapsing), 10 years of political and economic instability followed, culminating in a bloodless "Rose Revolution", leading to the establishment of a liberal democracy and a realignment with NATO and western europe (this has led to tensions with putin)

Georgia has two primary political parties, United National Movement, a center-right party that led the Rose Revolution, was described as "perhaps the freest market government in the world" and a "neoliberal autocracy", and held power until 2007, and again, briefly in 2012, and the Georgian Dream party, a mashup of disparate political ideologies -- it was founded by a billionaire businessman, supports whatever is politically opportune -- they're culturally slightly center left, and economically neoliberal with a safety, and made Georgia more lgbt-friendly

now one of the former UNM prime ministers named Saakashvili was arrested in 2014 for abusing the office of president (arresting and killing a couple of innocent people) and embezzling government money. He was recently sentenced to 6 years in prison. This article is just "Georgian Dream won the election pretty definitively (46% to 30%), and Saakashvili has returned from exile to serve his 6 year prison sentence, which the ruling party supports"
