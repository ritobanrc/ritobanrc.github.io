# Reread #1
I really need to get better at doing this the day after I finish the book. It's been nearly 5 days now since I finished it. Anyway, hopefully I haven't forgotten too much. 

This was _far_ better than Way of Kings for the majority of the book. As usual, since it was Sanderson, I already know it's amazing, so I'm going to focus on the parts that I didn't like, or the parts that stood out to me as extra well done. Unfortunately, unlike Way of Kings, I simply didn't feel the overwhelming rush of emotion at the end -- I just didn't care that much about Kaladin's fight with Szeth. It was honestly kinda boring.

I was particularly annoyed at Shallan this book. She was a complete Mary Sue protagonist. In WoK, I said that it almost felt like she was satirizing a YA protagonist - extremely full of herself, overly sarcastic, but kept failing. In this book, she was literally just magically good at whatever she wanted. Infiltrate a highly secretive extremely dangerous potentially criminal organization? Check! Baffle and perfectly play politics with the most powerful people in the kingdom? Check! Magically know how to use a shardblade to efficiently escape from the chasmfiend? Check! Magic drawing abilities also let you create a perfect map saving the day? Check! Be able to convince a band of deserters and slavers to follow you and do your bidding? Check!

However, the pacing was much better than WoK. There were a couple parts that grated -- Shallan and Kaladin's time in the chasm felt forced (Shaladin was never a thing), Shallan in the Frostlands was kinda boring, but for most of it, I was overwhelmingly extremely excited to read what was going to come next. I pretty much didn't do anything but read for several days straight.

There were also several places where the prose in this book was significantly better than WoK. It was still pretty terse, but there were a handful of beautifully written sentences here and there. 

The themes here are _sooooo_ much better developed than WoK. I can't believe I ever entertained the idea that the whole "if only everyone acted honorable, then society would fix itself" shtick was fascistic. I really love the "I'll do it, cause someone has to step up for what is right." -- Jodi tor dak shune keu na ashe, tobe ekla cholo re! I still don't buy the "Elhokar is Dalinar's Tien" thing, cause like... Elhokar is the fucking king. He has a fucking army at his disposal. He's not totally defenseless. But I'll digress at that point.

There was also some really neat symbolism here. The whitespine was particularly well executed. Brandon had me guessing about who the whitespine was all the way up to the duel--is it Amaram? Kaladin? Dalinar? And at some level, it works for all of them, and that was lovely.

Overall, far, far better than WoK, definitely deserves a very high rating.
