fuckin' hell eighty-seven percent of lobbying is from businesses
the largest spenders on politics are the US chamber of commerce, followed by the national association of realtors, a big pharma company, american hospital association, blue cross (the insurance company), amazon and facebook
no shit we can't get medicare for all or housing reform or tech regulation passed in this country
those three industries are literally the biggest spenders in lobbying
and its honestly pretty evenly split democrats and republicans
pharma companies in particular are even, perhaps slightly liberal
financial lobbyists are pretty even
law firms are solidly democratic
insurance, with the exception of one major republican lobbying firm, is evenly split democratic republican
hedge funds are also evenly split
hey look the largest chunk of amazon's lobbying is on issues of "labor, anti-trust, and workplace"
damn the single largest interest group in the 2020 election cycle was "retired" people -- and they mostly donated to kelly loeffler
the next group, lawyers/law firms doesn't even come close, by a factor of 3
oh and all the money that retired people spent is a drop in the bucket compared to what the US chamber of commerce spends
hell even defense lobbying is evenly split, republican/democratic -- both parties are literally identical in their foreign policy
