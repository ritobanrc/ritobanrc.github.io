we live in a civilized country, vigilante or mob justice is generally frowned upon, and i think that's a good thing -- albeit, twitter cancellation is hardly any kind of justice, its entirely impactless, and its also hardly a mob, its entirely manufactured by the twitter algorithm
like that second point i think is more important -- "cancellation" is not anything resembling doling out justice. if it was, the people being "cancelled" would be the politicians who keep accepting bribes or keep voting to increase the military budget. cancellation is phenomenon entirely manufactured by the twitter algorithm to maximize your time on the site on your interaction with the content on the site, because that lets them maximize the number of ads you see. that is it. that's why the topics are so trivial -- if they were more complicated, then people couldn't reply instantly and get into heated arguments as quickly, which isn't as good for the algorithm. that's also why the topics are so simple and clean cut -- there's two sides, you can immediately pick a side, and immediately start arguing, and that's what the algorithm wants you to do
like I don't give much of a shit about James Charles, because he was one individual, and I can't imagine that whoever his victims were actually give much of a shit about whether some people make angry twitter posts about him -- they probably care if he goes to prison for it, or if he faces any other real consequences, but that will be handled by the district attorney
like I feel like this is the shit that lefties make up for themselves to make themselves feel like they're having an impact, cause realizing that you don't have any power is depressing. so you say "we're keeping the powerful elite accountable!", when in reality, no you're not, you're posting shit on twitter about individuals who aren't really using their power to do much harm (like james charles abusing even 10  minors is virtually irrelevant in comparison to the effect that even a minor politician like patricia lock dawson can have in instructing riverside police to conduct sweep of homeless encampments)
and the twitter algorithm has realized that feeling of powerlessness exists, and has tapped into it, creating the phenomenon we call "cancellation"
