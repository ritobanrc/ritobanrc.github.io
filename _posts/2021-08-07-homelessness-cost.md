title: Cost of Solving Homelessness
tags: [politics]
---

I've seen several quite low estimates for the "cost to solve homeless", usually around as low as \$1b. 

i suspect they arrived at that estimate by taking the number of homeless people in the US (500k) and multiplying by \$2000 (average monthly rent in most large cities)
(the idea being that once you put someone in a house, they can find a job and probably start paying rent on their own)
obviously there are a lot of problems with that estimate -- (1) that 500k figure is probably an undercount, since estimating the number of homeless people is hard, so a lot of states don't even bother counting them (that's why mississippi has the lowest homeless population of any state, despite having one of the highest poverty rates), (2) there are a lot of people who don't technically qualify as homeless, but are struggling with paying rent, which is part of "the housing crises", if not homelessness, (3) obviously everyone being able to find a job that's good enough to pay \$2k in rent per month within the first month is quite ambitious and at least somewhat unlikely, and (4) there are probably gonna be some market effects of that as well -- if a whole bunch of people show up looking for somewhat unskilled jobs that pay around 2k/month, that's gonna drive the cost of labor down, making it harder to find jobs, and if a whole bunch of cheap rentals are bought up by the government in a short period of time, that's probably also going to drive up the cost of rentals, making it harder for other people to get houses

none of those problems are insurmountable, but its probably going to cost a bit more than \$1 billion -- if nothing else, solving homelessness probably requires solving "the housing crises" more broadly, and that needs to be a combination of government constructed social housing (which america doesn't like for some reason, cause it looks socialist), and that housing probably needs to be made available to a lot more than just the 500k homeless people that are counted regularly, it needs to be made available to everyone who struggles with rent
