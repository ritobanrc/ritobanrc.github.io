Write to your representative here: www.govtrack.us/congress/bills/117/hr1019

Dear Representative Takano,

I am calling on you to cosponsor H.R. 1019, the E-BIKE Act. The Southern California area is famously one of the most car-dependent places in all of America, with many roads lacking even a basic sidewalk. Unfortunately, a car is an incredibly large expense for many families, leading this Harvard study (cited in the New York Times) to conclude that transportation is the single largest barrier to escaping poverty: www.nytimes.com/2015/05/07/upshot/transportation-emerges-as-crucial-to-escaping-poverty.html, and similar impacts have been observed by the  NHTS (nhts.ornl.gov/briefs/PovertyBrief.pdf), estimating that households in poverty spend a disproportionate portion of their income on transportation expenses, and this burden disproportionately falls on people of color.  

The negative effects of car dependence have been extensively chronicled in the academic literature, with effects ranging from public health and social isolation to economic stagnation and worsening racial and economic inequality. 

www.strongtowns.org/journal/2015/1/20/the-negative-consequences-of-car-dependency
www.planetizen.com/blogs/111535-automobile-dependency-unequal-burden
https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10900-013-9681-1

Fortunately, Riverside, and many other cities in the inland Empire have begun to improve this, slowly, but surely making our cities more walkable and bikeable. The new separated bike lanes in Riverside -- on Iowa Ave, Canyon Crest, and Watkins Drive are a very good start, though similar projects need to be installed throughout the city. Crucially, for Riverside, because its density is so low, journeys tend to be considerably longer, at least 3-4 miles even for short trips to the grocery store, and often 10-12 miles for a commute into downtown. These distances are often too long for regular bikes or informal bike commuters, but are ideal for E-BIKES, which provide pedal assist for riders, allowing them to travel considerably longer distances without having to exert as much physical effort, making biking a much more viable option for practical transportation. Indeed, in the last several years, the number of E-BIKE sales has exploded, outpacing even electric car sales recently. 

With climate change bearing down on us faster than we can imagine, and an impressive infrastructure bill to help combat it, further encouraging alternative forms of transportation is absolutely critical, and their recent popularity strongly suggests that E-BIKEs are bound to be a significant contributor to transportation in the future. Therefore, I call on you to co-sponsor the E-BIKE Act, which would subsidize E-BIKE purchases by 30%, providing a much-needed incentive for further E-BIKE adoption. 

Thank you for your time.
