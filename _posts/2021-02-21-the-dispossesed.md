It's been a couple days since I finished _The Dispossessed_ (I'm writing this 21 Feb), but it really is quite an interesting book. Let's get through the writing quickly, since that's the comparatively boring part. The interesting part of this book are the themes.

## Story

The story was ok. There was definitely an interesting element of mystery, waiting to find out what caused Shevek to come to Urras -- but admittedly, it was never _that_ much of a mystery. Shevek's experiences on Urras were mostly repetitive, him getting shocked at both the opulence and the destitution. There were times when my eyes glazed over while reading this. Nonetheless, the story presented an interesting premise, and that mostly kept me going.

There were a couple of really great scenes -- Takver giving birth the second time was truly terrifying, Shevek at the protest coming to the realization that the purpose of the military was to allow innocent people to kill each other.
