title  : A Wizard of Earthsea
tags: [book-review]
---

author : Ursula LeGuin
start  : 27 Jun 2020
end    :  9 Jul 2020
rating : 4.0

It's been well over a week since I finished this book, I just kinda kept forgetting to write this, so I don't really remember all the details.

There was quite a bit that I liked here. In the afterword, LeGuin talks about how she wanted to write a fantasy story that didn't involve war, and she definitely achieved that quite well. The story is structured as a bildungsroman, a coming of age story, and that definitely works. She also has some really great symbolism here. I especially love the way to plays with seasons to complement and build up the atmosphere of certain scenes. The prose was also quite nice, but often, it was just a bit too flowery for me, even feeling a bit choppy. Sometimes, it flowed really well, but at other times, I found myself having to reread a couple of sentences. Unfortunately, I've forgotten a lot of my immediate thoughts after finishing the book, but I definitely hope to continue this series eventually.
