---
title: Numeric In-Text Citations are Horrible
tags: [rant]
---

i've also grown to hate number-based citation formats like [5]

* they're horrible for so many reasons -- I know why [Zhu and Bridson 2005] is -- its the incompressible FLIP paper from 2005 -- I know who Zhu and Bridson are, they're other papers, etc. I do not know anything about [5]. To figure out what [5] is, I have to scroll down to the bibliography, read the citation, realize that "oh its just the incompressible FLIP paper i've already read", scroll back up, and I've gained absolutely nothing in the process
* further, to use citations like [5], you must use latex. there's no way around it -- if you use anything else, when you add a citation, you will have to scroll through your entire paper and change every single citation, and you will mess that up. and if you use latex, what you're putting in your code is `\citep{zhu2005flip}` anyway -- so your citation might as well look similar
* and finally, its grammatically horrible -- "We use the same scheme as [5]." just sounds bad, there's no situation in which a number citation sounds good. Just say "We use the same scheme as Zhu and Bridson [2005]", which is a perfectly grammatical and reasonable sentence. A lot of authors already do that, "We use the same scheme as Zhu and Bridson [5]", but that defeats the whole point of using the numbers cause they're concise, and conveys less information!
