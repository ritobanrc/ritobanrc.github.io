title: Complaining about Right Wing Themes in Fantasy

there are so many right wing fantasy fans
they're usually like larpers who wanna imagine a world that only has white men and is free from any modern complexities and is just generally socially regressive
i've told you about r/whitecloaks right
oh its a group of people who are _very_ mad at the wheel of time tv show cause it has non-white people
ironically, they named themself after the whiteclocks, which in the book, is literally a KKK-esque/Spanish Inquisition religious fanatic organization
they literally go around arresting people without cause and killing them

there's like terry goodkind esque fantasy where the character just stops and starts making an ayn rand style speech about how great he is every couple of pages and goes around raping women then justifying it with "they wanted it"

there's george rr martin fantasy where your convinced that the author was horny af while writing it

hell -- aragorn's arc in LOTR is literally "he has better blood because he is descended from the rightful king, we just need to find the people with the right heritage and society will be great"

another example -- patrick rothfuss, wise man's fear, a non-trivial part of the book is our young (presumably white, male) protagonist losing his virginity to a literal sex goddess who has sex with him for days on end, and then going to another "eccentric", vaguely east easian society where having sex with mentors is seen as normal cause it "allows students to focus better" -- its literally a 4chan incel fantasy, or some bizarre porn plot 

afoundation is like super widely respected as one of the OG sci-fi stories -- personally, the story bored the crap out of me, its written as a series of 5 short stories, and i didn't get to know the characters in any of them -- but the thing that stuck out to me most was the frankly bizarre lack of female characters -- there is not a single female character in 90% of the book. there is one scene, with a female character who is the wife of a queen, and she is literally only on screen for a couple of minutes for the protagonist to gift her some jewelery as a token of friendship, followed by her being kicked out of the room.

brandon sanderson is often really good with the politics in his books -- the discussion of classism, of slavery in stormlight is spectacular, there's this really hierarchical class system where different people are sorted into different castes, and the upper castes are people with light eyes, and the lower caste are people with dark eyes (obviously a commentary on how arbitrary racism is). mistborn is also pretty good -- once again, there's two castes, nobles and skaa, the skaa are prevented from rising up against nobles through a bunch of things, they have to work in the mines which keeps the society running really great. at the end of the first book, the evil big bad dictatorial lord ruler is overthrown, and something resembling a bourgeouis revolution takes place (there's clear inspiration from both the english and french revolutions), which institutes something like a constitutional monarchy where a monarch is elected-ish by a group primarily made of merchants and shit. and this is portrayed as a good thing, our protagonist's husband is the first elected monarch, very cool -- except literally the entire plotline of the next too books is that monarch complaining about the democratic restraints that he helped build preventing him from actually dealing with the threats they face, and doing everything to undermine the democracy from the inside, as his badass assassin wife goes around murdering people who disagree with him! 

and keep in mind, i like most of these books (with the exception of terry goodkind and foundation) -- but you can't deny that fantasy has some really frickin weird themes in it sometimes
