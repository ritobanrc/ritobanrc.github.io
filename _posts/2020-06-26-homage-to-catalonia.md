title  : Homage to Catalonia
tags: [book-review]

* Author : George Orwell
* Start  :  7 Jun 2020
* End    : 25 May 2020 
* Rating : 2.3

This book wasn't particularly good. It describes Orwell's time in Catalonia, but doesn't really provide a cohesive or coherent narrative throughout the story. Instead, it feels like a series of disconnected events. Broadly, a handful of ideas just keep getting repeated: 

- Life sucked in the trenches, worsened by the poor economic state of the revolutionaries
- The soviet union and communists actively sought to undermine the revolution.
- But worker control and a lack of hierarchy could often be "felt"

The story sort of just repeats these three ideas several ideas in a disjoint fashion. Nor is Orwell's prose particularly engaging or enthralling -- in fact, he occasionally even shows an ironic self awareness, saying "I cannot describe the feeling" or something to that effect. 

Frankly, the primary purpose for him writing this book seems to be to simply to present _any_ evidence contrary to the Spanish, English, and Soviet propaganda, because as he establishes, there is no shortage of it.
